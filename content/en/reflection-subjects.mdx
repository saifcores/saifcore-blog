Reflection articles are **opinion and experience**, not tutorials. They answer _why_ and _what it felt like_, complementing ADRs and code guides. Use them on LinkedIn for reach; on the blog for depth; link always to a portfolio case study or contact.

<Callout variant="info">
  Format: 800–1 500 words, one clear thesis, one personal or observed example,
  one actionable takeaway. Tag as `kind: reflection` when published.
</Callout>

## Craft & engineering identity

1. **The loneliness of the payment engineer** — Only you see the ledger, the callback, and the support ticket at 2 a.m.; why specialization in money systems is undervalued until something breaks.
2. **Seniority is saying no to the wrong abstraction** — When a “quick SDK wrapper” becomes your core product forever.
3. **Debugging money is debugging trust** — Every ambiguous state is a future dispute with finance, ops, or a regulator.
4. **Why I still draw diagrams before writing code** — Communication as a deliverable, not a meeting afterthought.
5. **The best code I shipped was the integration we cancelled** — Reflection on scope, risk, and the cost of “just one more provider.”

## Africa & local context

6. **Building from Dakar for global rails** — Distance from HQs as clarity, not handicap.
7. **When your user’s bank is a phone number** — Product assumptions that break outside card-first markets.
8. **Copy-paste Silicon Valley is a liability** — What to keep (discipline, testing) vs what to discard (settlement timing, UX defaults).
9. **French + English in the same codebase, the same stand-up** — Bilingual delivery as an architecture and team design problem.
10. **Infrastructure optimism vs operational reality** — Uptime on slides vs power, connectivity, and partner outages in the field.

## Leadership & consulting

11. **Architecture reviews that changed my mind** — Stories where the reviewer was wrong, and where they saved a launch.
12. **The first 90 minutes of a fintech audit** — What I look at before touching a line of code.
13. **Consulting without hero mode** — Handoff, documentation, and leaving teams stronger than you found them.
14. **Saying “not yet” to a feature that moves money** — How to keep product trust while pushing back.
15. **Teaching stakeholders to love idempotency keys** — Translating engineering invariants into business language.

## Ethics & responsibility

16. **Who pays when the ledger is wrong?** — Moral weight of balance errors vs feature velocity.
17. **Designing for exclusion on purpose** — Limits, KYC, and friction as features, not bugs.
18. **Transparency as a competitive advantage in fintech** — Showing states users and finance can both understand.
19. **The audit trail you hope nobody ever needs** — Building for investigators, not demos.
20. **When “move fast” meets “move money”** — Personal lines I will not cross on shortcuts.
